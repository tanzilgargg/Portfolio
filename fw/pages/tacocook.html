Traditional street tacos are made with soft corn tortillas!

Back to Dish Tips & Tricks

Tacos

Key Ingredient

Corn

The corn tortilla is the foundation of a traditional taco. Made from masa harina (corn flour), tortillas are pressed thin and cooked on a hot griddle until they develop a slight char. Their slightly chewy texture and subtle corn flavor provide the perfect vessel for taco fillings, while their flexibility allows them to be folded without breaking.

Recipe

Prep: 15min
Cook: 20min
Total Time: 35min

INGREDIENTS

4-6 Servings

For the Taco Filling

  • 1 lb ground beef (80% lean)
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

For the Toppings

  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

PREPARATIONS

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Drain excess fat if necessary.

Step 2: Season the Meat

Add taco seasoning and water to the skillet. Stir well to combine. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens slightly.

Step 3: Warm the Tortillas

While the meat is simmering, warm the tortillas. You can do this by heating them in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds per side, or by wrapping them in foil and placing them in a 350°F oven for 5-10 minutes.

Step 4: Assemble the Tacos

To assemble each taco, place a warm tortilla on a plate. Add a spoonful of the meat mixture down the center. Top with shredded lettuce, cheese, diced tomatoes, and a dollop of sour cream. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with lime wedges on the side.
