Pizza Margherita

Key Ingredient

San Marzano Tomatoes

San Marzano tomatoes are the gold standard for authentic Neapolitan pizza sauce. Grown in the volcanic soil of Mount Vesuvius, these tomatoes have a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity with fewer seeds and less water than other varieties. Their thick flesh and rich flavor create the ideal base for Pizza Margherita, providing a vibrant red color and authentic taste that has been cherished for generations.

Recipe

Prep: 30min
Cook: 15min
Dough Rest: 24h
Total Time: 24h 45min

INGREDIENTS

4 Pizzas

For the Pizza Dough

  • 1kg (7 cups) Tipo 00 flour
  • 600ml (2.5 cups) lukewarm water
  • 20g (2 tbsp) fine sea salt
  • 3g (1 tsp) active dry yeast

For the Toppings

  • 800g (28 oz) San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 400g (14 oz) Mozzarella di Bufala, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fine sea salt

PREPARATIONS

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Dissolve yeast in lukewarm water and let stand for 5 minutes. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Gradually add the yeast water, stirring with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms. Knead for 10-15 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 24 hours. Remove from refrigerator 2 hours before using.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

Crush the San Marzano tomatoes by hand, removing any stems or hard parts. Season with a pinch of salt. No cooking is needed for authentic Neapolitan pizza sauce - the tomatoes will cook on the pizza.

Step 3: Shape and Top the Pizza

Preheat your oven to its highest temperature (ideally 500°F/260°C or higher) with a pizza stone if available. Divide dough into 4 equal portions. Stretch each portion into a 12-inch circle, leaving the edges slightly thicker. Place on a pizza peel dusted with flour. Spread a thin layer of crushed tomatoes, leaving a border. Add torn mozzarella pieces.

Step 4: Bake and Finish

Transfer pizza to the preheated oven and bake for 8-12 minutes until the crust is charred in spots and the cheese is melted. Remove from oven and immediately top with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Serve hot.
