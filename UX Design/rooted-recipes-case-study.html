📌 Project Overview

Rooted Recipes represents a conceptual shift in how we think about digital recipe platforms—moving beyond simple ingredient lists to embrace the rich cultural narratives, family traditions, and seasonal wisdom that make food truly meaningful.

The Challenge

Most recipe platforms prioritize speed and convenience over cultural authenticity and emotional connection. Users seeking to preserve family traditions or explore their heritage through food often find themselves frustrated by generic, westernized interpretations of traditional dishes.

This research aimed to understand how digital platforms can better serve users who value cultural authenticity, seasonal eating, and the stories behind their food.

❓ The Problem Space

Most online recipe sites focus on speed, trends, or aesthetics—rarely prioritizing heritage, cultural respect, or emotional connection to food.

1

Cultural Disconnect

Traditional recipes lose context and meaning when stripped of their cultural stories and family histories.

2

Seasonal Confusion

Users struggle to understand what ingredients are in season locally, disconnecting them from sustainable eating practices.

3

Lost Knowledge

Aging family members hold irreplaceable cooking wisdom that risks being lost without proper documentation.

The Opportunity

There's a significant gap in the market for a recipe platform that prioritizes cultural authenticity, storytelling, and community contribution over speed and convenience.

Users are hungry for meaningful food experiences that honor their heritage while helping them navigate modern challenges like ingredient sourcing and seasonal eating.

Key Insight: Food platforms have the potential to become cultural preservation tools, not just recipe repositories.

🛠️ Proposed Solutions

Based on our research insights, I designed five key features that address the core needs of users seeking authentic, culturally-rich cooking experiences.

"Grandma's Notes" Section

Every recipe includes a story-based introduction providing cultural context, family history, and emotional significance. Users can read about the dish's origins, traditional occasions when it's served, and personal memories from the contributor.

Map-Based Recipe Discovery

Explore authentic recipes by geographic region or diaspora communities. Users can discover dishes from specific cultural areas, understand regional variations, and connect with recipes from their ancestral homelands.

Seasonal Ingredient Calendar

Location-based seasonal ingredient recommendations help users source fresh, local ingredients for traditional recipes. The calendar shows what's in season, where to find specialty items, and suggests recipe adaptations based on availability.

Heritage Tag System

Community-driven database where users can upload recipes with cultural heritage tags. This creates an authentic repository of traditional recipes while allowing users to contribute their family's unique variations and stories.

Elder Audio Stories

Optional audio or video recordings from elders sharing recipe histories, cooking techniques, and cultural wisdom. This preserves oral traditions and adds authentic voices to the cooking experience.

🪞 Reflection & Impact

What I Learned

This project deepened my understanding of emotional UX and the critical role of storytelling in digital spaces. I discovered how data—both qualitative and quantitative—can guide not just design decisions, but ethical storytelling choices that respect cultural heritage.

The research revealed that food platforms have an untapped opportunity to become cultural preservation tools, not just recipe repositories. Users are seeking authenticity and connection in an increasingly digital world.

Next Steps

If expanded, this project would benefit from co-creation workshops with elders and cultural food experts to ensure authentic representation. I would also conduct usability testing of the proposed features and explore partnerships with cultural organizations.

The ultimate goal would be to create a platform that not only serves recipes but actively participates in cultural preservation and community building.

Food is memory, culture, and connection. Digital platforms have the power to honor and preserve these sacred relationships—if designed with empathy, cultural sensitivity, and deep respect for the communities they serve.
