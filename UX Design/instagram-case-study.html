Understanding the Instagram User Experience

A comprehensive UX research study exploring user behaviors, pain points, and unmet needs

My Role

Solo UX Researcher

Duration

2.5 Weeks (April 2025)

Platform

Instagram (Mobile and Web)

Project Overview

Instagram is a widely-used social platform, yet many users experience a range of emotions during use, from entertainment to anxiety. This UX research project aimed to understand real-world user behaviors, pain points, and unmet needs to provide data-backed recommendations for improving the Instagram experience.

The research focused particularly on Gen Z, daily users, and digital creators who form a significant portion of Instagram's user base and are most affected by the platform's design decisions.

Problem + Goals

Despite its popularity, Instagram continues to cause emotional fatigue and confusion among its user base. Features such as the algorithm, account privacy settings, and business tools frequently emerge as confusing or misaligned with user expectations.

Project Goals:

  • Explore user motivations and emotional responses to Instagram usage
  • Identify pain points across features like Stories, Reels, Explore, and DMs
  • Uncover unmet user needs to inform future design improvements

"Instagram is a platform that millions use daily, yet the emotional impact of its design choices remains understudied. This research aims to bridge that gap."

Research Plan

To gather insights, I launched a large-scale survey targeting active and occasional Instagram users across multiple age groups. The questions addressed usage habits, emotional responses, frustrations, and feature expectations.

Methodologies

  • Screener Survey (n = 70+ responses)
  • Thematic Analysis for open-ended responses
  • Quantitative Data Analysis for usage patterns and ratings
  • Persona Development based on key user types

Questions Asked

  • How often do you use Instagram?
  • What features do you use most frequently?
  • What parts of Instagram feel confusing or frustrating?
  • How does Instagram make you feel after use?
  • Have you ever taken a break from the platform? Why?
  • What is one thing you wish Instagram would fix?
  • What new feature would you add?

Key Findings

User Quotes

"I feel drained after scrolling for too long."

"Instagram hides posts; I want to refresh it manually."

"I couldn't find a post I saw earlier—there should be a history."

"I just want to post a reel from my private account, but it's confusing."

Pain Points

  • Lack of clarity in account and privacy settings
  • Over-personalized algorithm and irrelevant content
  • Invasive ads based on external browsing
  • Missing features like a content history tab
  • Emotional pressure tied to posting, comparing, or managing appearance

Emotional Themes

  • Drained or anxious after extended use
  • FOMO and performance pressure when not posting
  • Reluctance to post due to fear of judgment
  • Preference for passive browsing without interaction

Insights & Opportunities

Many users crave greater control over their content experience, particularly with the algorithm.

Emotional well-being is a key factor in usage behavior—users seek more mindful tools.

Younger users often want features that allow them to engage passively, without pressure to post or share publicly.

Creators feel underserved by current promotion tools and tracking systems.

Proposed Solutions

Rewatch History Feature

Enables users to revisit recently viewed Reels and Stories, addressing the frustration of losing content they enjoyed.

Digital Wellbeing Mode

Includes reminders, usage stats, mood check-ins, and focused usage limits to help users maintain a healthier relationship with the platform.

Custom Feed Filters

Let users create categories like "Chill," "Inspo," "Study Mode" for better feed organization and content control.

Reflection + Lessons Learned

This project showed the emotional complexity behind what appears to be routine app usage. While Instagram is heavily used, it doesn't always serve user well-being. I learned how to translate open-ended feedback into actionable insights, and how to identify overlooked feature opportunities.

If continued, this project would benefit from one-on-one interviews and usability testing of proposed designs. The next steps would involve creating high-fidelity prototypes and conducting A/B testing to validate the solutions.

"The most valuable insight from this research was understanding that users don't just want new features—they want more control over their existing experience."
