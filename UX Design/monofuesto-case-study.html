A culturally immersive, festival-themed board game experience that bridges traditional gameplay with modern mechanics

Project Overview

Monofuesto is a culturally immersive, festival-themed board game inspired by the structure of Monopoly but reimagined to engage modern players. The goal was to create a hybrid board game experience that respects traditional gaming principles while introducing culturally rich content and modern mechanics like electronic banking and a dynamic month-based spinner system.

Problem + Goals

Problem:

While traditional board games like Monopoly maintain strong nostalgic value, they often lack cultural representation, adaptability for younger audiences, and gameplay that integrates both traditional and contemporary experiences.

"Classic board games are beloved but often fail to represent diverse cultural experiences or adapt to modern gaming expectations."

Goals:

  • Create an engaging, culturally rooted board game experience.
  • Maintain the structured, rule-driven integrity of traditional board games.
  • Introduce innovative mechanics like "game flipping" between Traditional and Modern sides without overwhelming players.
  • Ensure clear rules, intuitive board actions, and high replay value.

Research Plan

Methodologies

  • Competitor Analysis: Monopoly, Ticket to Ride, Festival of Colors, Unstable Unicorns.
  • User Interviews: Semi-structured interviews with casual and avid board game players.
  • Live Playtesting: Multiple rounds of observation sessions, both moderated and unmoderated.
  • Surveys: Quantitative feedback from post-playtest forms.
  • Heuristic Evaluation: Usability evaluation based on traditional UX heuristics.

Questions Asked

  • How easy was it to understand the game's rules and objectives?
  • At what points did you feel confused during gameplay?
  • How engaging was the cultural theme for you personally?
  • What was your emotional experience flipping from the Traditional to the Modern side?
  • Would you recommend this game to others? Why or why not?

Quantitative & Qualitative Summary

Quantitative:

  • 85% rated final game clarity as "Excellent" (vs. 40% in early versions).
  • 90% of players enjoyed the cultural theme integration.
  • 70% said they would replay the game with new players.

Qualitative:

  • Strong emotional connection to festival representations.
  • Confusion around specific board spaces ("Metro Hub," "Lost and Found") initially.
  • Players loved the unpredictability introduced by the Month Spinner.

Findings

User Quotes

"The board feels so alive — it's like a celebration while playing."

"I wasn't sure what to do when I landed on Metro Hub... the instructions weren't obvious."

"Flipping the board midway made it feel like two games in one — that was super cool!"

Pain Points

  • Lack of immediate clarity for some special board spaces.
  • First-time players found flip timing slightly ambiguous.
  • Some Masala Moment cards needed simpler language for easier in-game understanding.

Emotional Themes

  • Nostalgia for family board games mixed with excitement over cultural representation.
  • Sense of adventure and unpredictability with month-based spinner.
  • Joy derived from cultural storytelling through festivals.

Insights & Opportunities

  • Clearer onboarding is critical, especially around new board mechanics (e.g., Month Spinner outcomes, Metro Hub teleportation).
  • Players valued the emotional storytelling behind each festival more than abstract gameplay mechanics.
  • Hybrid board mechanics (flip from Traditional to Modern) created a strong novelty effect — future board designs should consider dynamic evolutions during play.

User Flow

Start Game ➔ Spin Month ➔ Move ➔ Land on Space ➔ Draw Card/Perform Action ➔ Manage Money ➔ Flip Game Side ➔ End Game

Proposed Solutions

Professional Game Manual

Developed a comprehensive game manual inspired by Monopoly, ensuring clarity, brevity, and tradition.

Mini Instruction Cards

Introduced quick reference cards for unique spaces like Metro Hub and Lost & Found to reduce confusion during gameplay.

Spinner Quick Reference

Created a visual guide to reduce confusion during monthly spins and clarify outcomes for each month.

Card Language Refinement

BEFORE: "Proceed to acquire cultural tokens from the nearest festival celebration, contingent upon availability."

AFTER: "Take 2 festival tokens from the nearest celebration space if available."

Reflection + Lessons Learned

This project solidified my understanding of how small UX adjustments — such as clearer microcopy or simplified player actions — can dramatically improve user satisfaction. It also reinforced the importance of respecting traditional structures while innovating thoughtfully to meet modern user expectations.

"Ultimately, Monofuesto became a bridge between traditional gaming nostalgia and fresh cultural storytelling — a project that reflects my passion for user-centered design grounded in respect for heritage."

If I were to repeat this process:

  • I would incorporate iterative testing earlier in the prototype phase.
  • I would explore a companion digital app for banking and spinner tracking to further streamline the experience.
