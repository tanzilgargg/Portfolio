Systems Design Autonomous Agents Challenge

New Hire Welcome Agent

An autonomous onboarding flow that greets every new teammate on day zero and assigns a buddy with fair rotation.

Role UX Researcher & Agent Designer
Duration 1 Day
Tools Power Automate • Dataverse • Logic Flows

Project Overview

As part of the Microsoft Power Up Program’s Autonomous Agents Challenge, I designed a lightweight agent that detects new hire records in Dataverse and immediately sends a personalized welcome while assigning an onboarding buddy or manager. The focus: translate HR’s “day‑one” ritual into clear, auditable logic without adding work for people ops.

Problem & Goals

The Problem

New teammates often experience uncertainty on day one because initial contact is inconsistent and support owners aren’t always assigned on time. HR bandwidth makes “nice‑to‑have” steps slip.

Goals

  • Trigger immediate, warm welcome on record creation.
  • Guarantee every new hire has a named support person.
  • Rotate assignments fairly to avoid overload.
  • Reduce manual HR effort and variability.

Research Plan

Methodologies

  • HR workflow mapping
  • Automation feasibility review
  • Trigger → action prototyping
  • Dataverse schema alignment

Key Questions

  • What wording feels genuinely welcoming?
  • How to detect new records reliably?
  • What’s a fair buddy rotation strategy?
  • Which channel scales but stays personal?

Insights & Opportunities

Day‑one sets culture

Warm, timely contact nudges trust and morale.

Agent ≠ cold

Automation can still feel human with tone and context.

Rotation = fairness

Even distribution protects teammates from overload.

Reflection & Lessons Learned

Key Learnings

  • Agents should sound human, not robotic.
  • Small, reliable touchpoints change day‑one sentiment.
