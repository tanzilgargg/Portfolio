Interactive Recipe Website

A personalized culinary platform that filters recipes based on user preferences and supports global cuisine exploration

Project Overview

As part of the Interactive Media course at OCAD University, I developed a responsive, interactive recipe website aimed at providing personalized culinary recommendations. Users begin by answering a series of preference-based questions (e.g., flavor profile: sweet, spicy, mild, etc.). Based on their inputs, the system filters through a database of recipes and displays a customized selection with clear instructions, ingredients, and contextual information about the dish.

The platform also enables users to explore global cuisines by region and submit their own recipes, fostering a collaborative food discovery experience.

Problem & Goals

Problem

Traditional recipe websites often overwhelm users with too many choices and lack a personalized approach to food discovery.

"I'm tired of scrolling through ten tabs just to find something spicy and simple."

Goals

To design a user-centered, interactive recipe platform that filters suggestions based on taste preferences and regional interests while supporting user-generated content and feedback.

Research Plan

Methodologies

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated platforms such as Yummly and Tasty to identify strengths and gaps in current recipe platforms

User Interviews

Conducted 1:1 interviews with five users (ages 18‚Äì30) to understand their recipe discovery habits

Preference Surveys

Collected input on taste-based filtering vs. traditional search methods

Usability Testing

Validated interaction flow using low-fidelity prototypes

Sample Questions Asked

  • How do you currently search for recipes online?
  • What motivates your food choices (taste, region, occasion)?
  • Would you feel encouraged to submit your own recipe? Why or why not?
  • What's missing from your current recipe-finding experience?

Quantitative & Qualitative Summary

  • 80% preferred filtering by taste over ingredient or prep time
  • 100% expressed interest in exploring global cuisines by region
  • 60% were open to contributing personal recipes if the process was simple
  • Visual hierarchy and clarity were cited as critical for user engagement

Key Findings

User Quotes

"I'm tired of scrolling through ten tabs just to find something spicy and simple."

"It would be nice to get suggestions based on what I feel like eating, not what I have."

"I'd love to add my grandma's recipes if there was an easy upload option."

Pain Points

  • Generic filtering tools on most platforms
  • Lack of emotional or cultural engagement
  • Time-consuming user flows

Emotional Themes

  • Users seek convenience without sacrificing personal connection
  • Emotional resonance found in regional identity and family recipes
  • Desire to be part of a community-driven culinary space

Proposed Solutions

Preference-Based Filtering

Quiz-style interface that asks users about their taste preferences and dietary needs to provide personalized recommendations.

Regional Cuisine Explorer

Interactive map interface allowing users to discover authentic recipes from different regions around the world.

Community Recipe Submission

Simplified form for users to share family recipes with cultural context and personal stories.

Visual Design Approach

  • Clean, single-column layout for recipe instructions
  • Visual category blocks on homepage for quick navigation
  • Quiz-style taste preference form with intuitive options
  • Scrollable recipe cards with preview thumbnails

Reflection & Lessons Learned

This project provided hands-on experience in bridging UX principles with front-end development. I applied JavaScript's .filter() method to structure dynamic interactions based on real user preferences‚Äîa key insight from our coursework. Feedback from the professor helped shape the object-oriented approach to recipe data and reinforced the importance of modular design logic.

"I gained valuable understanding of user behavior when engaging with food content online, especially the emotional undercurrent behind culinary preferences."

Key Takeaways

  • Food discovery is deeply personal and emotional‚Äîdesign should reflect this
  • Filtering by preference rather than ingredients creates a more intuitive experience
  • Cultural context adds significant value to recipe content
  • Community contribution features need to be simple to encourage participation

In future iterations, I aim to deepen accessibility support and test more robust search functionality.
