EdTech Platform Homepage Design

Designing an engaging and functional homepage for young learners from Pre-K to Class 6

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

August 2025

Platform

Web Application

Project Overview

This project focused on designing a homepage for an EdTech web application targeting children from Pre-K to Class 6. The primary goal was to create an interface that balances engagement with functionality, ensuring young learners can enjoy their educational journey while navigating intuitively through the platform.

The design adhered to specified brand guidelines while incorporating child-computer interaction principles to create an age-appropriate learning environment that supports both independent exploration and parental oversight.

Problem + Goals

Designing for young learners presents unique challenges: varying reading abilities, short attention spans, and the need for interfaces that are both educational and entertaining. The platform needed to accommodate children with different developmental stages while maintaining educational focus.

Design Challenges:

  • Creating intuitive navigation for early readers with limited text comprehension
  • Balancing visual engagement without overwhelming young users
  • Ensuring accessibility across different developmental stages and abilities
  • Maintaining educational focus while incorporating playful, motivating elements

"The interface must speak the language of children—visual, intuitive, and encouraging—while supporting their educational growth and development."

Research Plan

To create an effective design for young learners, I conducted comprehensive research into child-computer interaction principles, competitive analysis, and accessibility guidelines specific to educational platforms.

Methodologies

  • Competitive Analysis of EdTech platforms (BYJU's, Khan Academy Kids, Duolingo Kids)
  • Child-Computer Interaction (CCI) principles research
  • Color psychology and typography studies for young users
  • Accessibility guidelines analysis for children with varying abilities

Key Research Questions

  • How do children navigate digital interfaces differently than adults?
  • What visual cues help early readers understand navigation and functionality?
  • How can we balance engagement with educational focus without distraction?
  • What accessibility considerations are crucial for Pre-K to Class 6 age group?
  • How do familiar interaction patterns reduce cognitive load for young users?

Research Insights

Key Discoveries

"Children rely heavily on icons and visual cues for navigation, especially early readers who may struggle with text-only interfaces."

"Progress indicators and achievement systems significantly increase motivation and sustained engagement."

"Familiar patterns from platforms like YouTube Kids reduce cognitive load and improve usability."

Design Implications

  • Dual-modal navigation combining icons with text labels
  • Large touch targets (minimum 44px) for developing motor skills
  • High contrast colors for improved readability
  • Progress bars and visual feedback for motivation
  • Rounded, friendly design elements to reduce intimidation

Competitive Analysis Insights

  • BYJU's: Content-rich but text-heavy, opportunity for visual simplification
  • Khan Academy Kids: Overly cartoonish, balance needed between playful and educational
  • Duolingo Kids: Effective gamification without educational distraction

Proposed Solutions

Child-Friendly Color System

Implemented a balanced palette using Caribbean Green (#00D486) and Golden Yellow (#FFDA00) as primary colors, creating vibrant energy without overwhelming young users.

Dual-Modal Navigation

Combined icons with text labels to support both visual recognition and reading development, making navigation accessible for varying literacy levels.

Progress & Feedback Systems

Integrated progress bars, star ratings, and achievement indicators to provide immediate feedback and maintain motivation throughout the learning journey.

Layout & Interaction Design

Banner Carousel

Grade-specific recommendations with automatic rotation to showcase different learning areas while maintaining user engagement.

Content Organization

Horizontal carousels for different categories (Courses, Books, Videos, Games) following familiar interaction patterns.

Accessibility Considerations

Inclusive Design Features

  • High Contrast Design: Ensured sufficient color contrast ratios for users with visual impairments
  • Large Touch Targets: Minimum 44px interactive areas for developing motor skills
  • Simple Navigation: Clear, predictable patterns that reduce cognitive load
  • Visual Hierarchy: Clear information architecture supporting different reading levels
  • Responsive Design: Optimized for various devices and screen sizes

"Accessibility considerations for children often benefit all users, creating more inclusive and intuitive interfaces that support diverse learning needs and abilities."

Reflection + Lessons Learned

This project reinforced the importance of designing with empathy and understanding the unique needs of different user groups. Working within the constraints of child-computer interaction principles challenged me to think beyond traditional design patterns and consider how developmental stages impact user experience.

Key takeaways include the critical role of visual hierarchy in supporting early readers, the power of familiar interaction patterns in reducing cognitive load, and the importance of balancing engagement with educational focus. The project also highlighted how accessibility considerations for children often benefit all users, creating more inclusive and intuitive interfaces.

"Moving forward, I would love to conduct user testing with actual children in the target age range to validate design decisions and gather insights that can only come from direct user feedback."
