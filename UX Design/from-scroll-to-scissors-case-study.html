From Scroll to Scissors

Bridging the Gap Between Hairstyle Inspiration and Stylist-Client Communication

Role

UX Researcher

Duration

4 weeks (part-time)

Tools

Zoom, Notion, Miro, Figma

Project Overview

This research project explored how clients discover hairstyle inspiration online and how that inspiration is communicated and interpreted by hairstylists. Despite the abundance of visual content across platforms like Pinterest and Instagram, miscommunications in the salon chair remain common. My goal was to understand these gaps and identify opportunities to create a more seamless, shared understanding between clients and stylists.

I led the end-to-end research process, from framing the problem and defining objectives to conducting interviews, synthesizing insights, and proposing user-centered solutions.

Problem + Goals

The Problem:

Clients often feel misunderstood when showing hairstyle references to stylists, leading to outcomes that don't match expectations. Meanwhile, stylists struggle to interpret vague visual cues, deal with missing context, or manage unrealistic demands.

Research Goals:

  • Understand how clients search for and choose hairstyle inspirations
  • Identify pain points in client-stylist communication around style preferences
  • Explore how stylists interpret and respond to inspiration references
  • Uncover opportunities to improve shared understanding through tools, language, or process

Pain Points

  • Inconsistent terminology between clients and stylists (e.g., "trim," "layers," "balayage")
  • Inspiration photos often lack context (e.g., hair type, maintenance needs)
  • Stylists spend extra time managing expectations and re-aligning goals
  • Clients lack confidence when communicating their preferences

Insights & Opportunities

Key Insights

  • Visual references alone are not enough — clients lack a structured way to express goals
  • Stylists want more context and collaboration before the appointment
  • Existing inspiration platforms aren't personalized to hair types or realistic outcomes

Opportunity Areas

  • Introduce a shared pre-consultation tool that allows both parties to align on expectations
  • Help clients filter inspiration by hair type, length, and style goals
  • Create a visual language system for common haircut terms (e.g., sliders, illustrations)

Proposed Solutions

💡 Concept: "StyleBridge" – a Pre-Appointment Hair Communication Assistant

A tool that bridges the gap between inspiration and consultation.

Key Features:

✨
Smart moodboard builder (with tags for texture, length, vibe)
👤
Hair type + face shape compatibility preview
📏
Style glossary with visual sliders (e.g., "length off," "layer type")
📝
Shared notes between stylist and client

Brief User Flow

  1. Client browses styles and builds a personalized moodboard
  2. System prompts questions like "What do you like about this?"
  3. Stylist receives the board before the appointment + comments
  4. Both review expectations together in-person or virtually

Reflection + Lessons Learned

This project taught me how deeply emotional even "simple" everyday experiences like haircuts can be. I learned that:

  • Communication design isn't just about interfaces — it's about trust and clarity
  • Stakeholder interviews reveal unspoken frustrations that surveys often miss
  • Visual communication needs shared context to avoid misalignment

If I expanded this project, I would:

  • Conduct more ethnographic observation in actual salon environments
  • Prototype and test the StyleBridge tool with real stylists and clients
  • Explore the cultural differences in hair communication across communities
